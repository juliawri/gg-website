Materials Required

You'll Need:

1 piece of string, beads in 3 colours, 1 binary key decoder found here.

How to Make Binary Bracelets

Step 1

Tie a knot on one end of the string.

Step 2

Find the first letter of your 1st name in the Binary Decoder Key. Choose a colour to represent the “on” or black spaces in your binary decoder key and another colour to represent the “off” or white spaces in your binary decoder. The binary decoder can be found here. The clear beads are for the spaces in between the letters.

Step 3

Place beads on your string based on the colour(s) you have assigned to 'on' and 'off' and attempt to spell the first letter of your name using binary. Continue with each letter of your name until you have run out of beads or finished your name.

Step 4

Tie the two ends of the string together.

Discussion Questions

What Does "Binary" Mean?

Binary is a way of representing information using only two options.

Can Computers Store Information in Binary?

Yes, computers can. Other objects can also do so as well. For example, CDs store information as either reflective or non- reflective.

How do Computers Communicate in Binary?

Computers have a lot of wires in them, which carry information through the computer and sometimes to other computers. Computers have only 2 options to communicate when they do so in binary: “on” or “off.”

How do Computers Convert Information into Binary?

Computers convert information we give them, such as stories we've written on the computer, into binary but using an agreed upon translating system. In this system, each letter is given a series "on" and "off" signals. The combination of these signals for each letter can be found here.
