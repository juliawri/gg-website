Materials Required

You'll Need:

1 paper towel roll, 1 pair of scissors, a small piece of cardstock, tape, 1 CD

How to Make a Spectroscope

Step 1

Cut a thin slit at a 45º angle toward the bottom of the cardboard tube.

Step 2

Create a small viewing hole on the other side of the cardboard tube, directly across from the slit.

Step 3

Trace one end of the paper towel roll onto cardstock and cut it out. Cut a straight slit across the center of the cardstock circle.Tape the circle onto the end end of paper towel roll without the viewing hole.

Step 4

Insert the CD into the slit in the paper towel roll with the shiny side facing up.

Discussion Questions

What is Light?

Light is a type of radiation (like what an X-ray machine uses) which is detectable by your eye. Light can come from many different places, such as the sun, stars, or lights in your room.

Is White a Colour?

No! Technically white is a shade instead of a colour. This is because white actually made of all the colours on the visible light spectrum.

What is a Spectrum?

The word “spectrum” often means a range but in science it can also mean a rainbow! This rainbow is made of all the colours the human eye can see. The rainbow is made when a white light ray is broken down into its components.

What are Spectroscopes?

A spectroscope splits light into its component wavelengths. This means that if a spectroscope was dealing with a white light, it would split the white light into the colours of the rainbow, because light contains the colours of the rainbow.

How Does my Spectroscope Work?

If you look closely you can see that your CD has small pits in it. These pits are evenly spaced so they break down the white light into the components from the rainbow. You can see this break down because the CD reflects the light.
